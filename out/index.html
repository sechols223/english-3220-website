About Me

I am Samuel Echols, a senior Computer Science student at Southeastern Louisiana University. My expertise spans software development and user-centered design. Motivated by studies on the local digital gap, I designed this project to empower our campus and surrounding communities.

The Challenge

Recent surveys show that nearly 30% of students and nearby households lack consistent high-speed internet. This limitation impacts remote learning, research access, and extracurricular engagement. Addressing this gap is critical for campus-wide inclusion.

Approach

  1. Network Infrastructure: Deploy high-speed Wi-Fi in campus libraries, study lounges, and community centers.
  2. Device Lending: Offer laptops, tablets, and peripherals through a streamlined checkout system.
  3. Instructional Workshops: Conduct sessions on essential software, cybersecurity best practices, and online research tools.
  4. Peer Support: Staff hubs with trained volunteers who provide one-on-one assistance and troubleshooting.
